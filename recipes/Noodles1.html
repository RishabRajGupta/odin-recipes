Chilled Lo Mein Noodles with Szechuan Peppercorns

Description

This recipe for chilled lo mein noodles with Szechuan peppercorns is a delicious and refreshing dish that is perfect for a hot summer day. The noodles are tossed in a flavorful sauce made with soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and chili oil, and then topped with toasted Szechuan peppercorns, garlic, ginger, scallions, and cilantro. The combination of flavors and textures is sure to impress your guests and leave them wanting more!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh Chinese egg noodles
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup chili oil
  • 1/4 cup Szechuan peppercorns
  • 1/4 cup minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup minced ginger
  • 1/4 cup minced scallions
  • 1/4 cup minced cilantro

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the noodles and cook according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and chili oil. Add the noodles and toss to coat.
  3. In a small skillet, toast the Szechuan peppercorns over medium heat until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Let cool, then grind in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Add the ground peppercorns, garlic, ginger, scallions, and cilantro to the noodles and toss to combine.
  5. Chill the noodles in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving.

Enjoy!
